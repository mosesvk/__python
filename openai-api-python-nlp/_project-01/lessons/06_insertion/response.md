AUDIT FINDING #3 — Accounts Receivable Reconciliation
Severity: Medium
Date Identified: April 13, 2026

OBSERVATION:
During our testing of accounts receivable transactions for the period ended March 31, 2026, we selected a sample of 40 transactions from the general ledger totaling $287,500. Our procedures included tracing these items to supporting invoices, delivery documentation, and subsequent cash receipts.

We identified the following exceptions:

• Eight invoices totaling $45,000 remained unreconciled to the accounts receivable subledger for periods exceeding 90 days
• Three items totaling $12,300 lacked supporting documentation in the AR aging report
• Two credit memos totaling $8,750 were recorded in the general ledger but not reflected in the subledger reconciliation
• The monthly reconciliation between the general ledger control account and the subledger was last performed on January 15, 2026, representing a two-month gap

Root Cause Analysis:
The primary cause of these exceptions is the absence of a formalized monthly reconciliation process. Currently, AR reconciliations are performed on an ad-hoc basis without documented procedures or supervisory review. This has resulted in timing differences and unresolved discrepancies accumulating without timely identification and correction.

Impact:
While no material misstatement was identified in the current period, the lack of preventive controls increases the risk of undetected errors, potential bad debt exposure, and inaccurate financial reporting in future periods.
RECOMMENDATION:
Management should implement a monthly reconciliation procedure with dual review
by the Controller and CFO prior to period close.
